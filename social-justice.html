Code For Progress

Inaugral Fellow (2014-2015)

1 of 12 Social Justice leaders selected out of 424 applicants to be in the inaugral cohort. Developed and applied full-stack Python/Django web development skills through a human-centred design framework. Designed a prototype for a web and mobile app called "There's A Job For That" to help people who are unemployed match their skillsets into other industries in need of employees. Presented app to Vice President Joe Bidden and Labor Secretary Thomas Perez.

The Social Justice Alliance (SJA)

Co-Founder, Core Organizer, and Curriculm Coordinator (2009-2011)

Developed and Co-Founded a Social Justice organization for student leaders passionate about advancing Social Justice issues in the Middlebury community and in systematically oppressed and underrepresented communities globally. Collaborated with faculty allies, administrators, student leaders and studen organizations to create a community taught Social Justice curriculum.

New Dawn Library Project

Fundraising Director (2011-2012)

Applied New leaders Fellowship training to design and launch fundraising campaign to build a library for the New Dawn Education Center in Nairobi, Kenya. Exceeded fundraising goal, generating over $25,000 to build a library, Chemistry/Physics lab, media center, books, furniture, and computers. Facilitated a Social Justice workshop with students, faculty, and volunteers at New Dawn.

Public Policy & International Affairs Fellowship

UC Berkeley Policy & Law Fellow (2008)

1 of 20 Junior undergrads selected from hundreds of applicants nationwide for a prestigious graduate school fellowship. Studied Policy and Quantitative Analysis at the Goldman School of Public Policy. PPIA is designed to prepare students working on social change initiatives in their community to pursue an education in public policy and law at a graduate level.

Anishinabe Academy
